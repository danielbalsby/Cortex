# Standard CDR checklist

Record positive and negative findings, evidence, and `N/E` for every untested item.

## Orientation and flow

- First impression communicates purpose, current patient/task context, and next action.
- Primary actions and clinical narrative are discoverable without instruction.
- Flow follows realistic clinical reasoning rather than database structure.
- Redundant decisions, duplicate entry, and avoidable confirmation are removed.
- Progressive disclosure preserves overview while revealing detail when needed.
- Click burden and scrolling are proportionate; repeated transitions do not interrupt thought.

## Interaction and access

- Keyboard navigation supports the full intended path with logical order and shortcuts where valuable.
- Focus is visible, predictable, and restored after dynamic changes.
- Errors identify the problem, preserve work, and support recovery.
- Responsive behaviour works at every supported viewport without hiding essential context or actions.

## Presentation

- Visual density supports scanning without excessive cards or whitespace.
- Typography is legible at supported sizes and zoom.
- Hierarchy makes patient context, positives, negatives, risks, decisions, and plan distinguishable.
- Colour and icons do not carry meaning alone.

## Clinical capture

- Grouped normal confirmation is efficient, transparent, and reversible.
- Positive overrides remain prominent and are not overwritten by grouped normals.
- Diagnostic suggestions are relevant, bounded, explainable, and never imply certainty.
- Clinical attention points are visible, actionable, and do not create alarm fatigue.
- Multiple concurrent working diagnoses are supported when the task requires them.

## Generated documentation

- Journal text accurately reflects selected positives, negatives, uncertainty, and corrections.
- Plan and referral language is specific enough for safe follow-up and does not invent actions.
- Untouched fields are omitted rather than documented as assessed or normal.
- Output ordering and terminology support later clinical reading.
- Editing or regeneration cannot silently discard or contradict clinically important content.

## Safety failure modes

- Test incomplete input, contradictory choices, rapid changes, refresh/back navigation, duplicate actions, timeout, and recoverable failure.
- Check unsafe defaults, hidden positives, stale state, wrong-patient/context risk, misleading normality, omission, and ambiguous provenance.
- Confirm blockers trigger stopping and escalation rules.
